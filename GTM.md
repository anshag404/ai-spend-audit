# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User (Persona)
- **Job Title**: VP of Engineering or Technical Co-founder.
- **Company Stage**: Seed to Series A (8–40 employees).
- **Hanging Out**: 
  - Subreddits: r/SaaS, r/startups, r/ycombinator.
  - Twitter: Following "build in public" accounts and AI infra leaders.
  - Slack Communities: YC Alumni, Launch House, local engineering leader groups.

## The Problem
Founders are seeing "AI sprawl." They have 5 developers on Cursor, 3 on ChatGPT Plus, and an OpenAI API bill that's spiking. Nobody is tracking the seat overlap or the missed annual discounts. The pain is felt monthly when the credit card statement arrives.

## First 100 Users (Total Budget: $0)
1. **Show HN (Hacker News)**: Launch with a highly transparent "Show HN" post. Focus on the "No Login" aspect to reduce friction.
2. **Cold Outreach to YC Batch List**: Use publicly available batch lists to send 20 personalized emails a day to CTOs. Subject: "Quick audit of [Company]'s AI spend?"
3. **Twitter "Value Thread"**: Post a thread analyzing the pricing models of the top 5 AI tools and how they are designed to make you overspend. Link the tool at the end.
4. **Indie Hackers Post**: Share the "Build Log" and focus on the technical implementation of the rule engine.

## Unfair Distribution Channel
**Strategic Partnership with Startup Accelerators**: By offering the tool as a "perk" or "mandatory checklist item" for new batch companies in accelerators like Techstars or 500 Startups, we get a direct line to companies at their most spend-heavy phase. Accelerators love this because it helps their portfolio companies extend their runway.

## Growth Loop
1. User completes audit.
2. User sees significant savings ($500+/yr).
3. User generates a shareable link to show their CEO/CFO how they are "optimizing the budget."
4. CEO/CFO sees the tool, trusts it, and recommends it to other founder friends.
