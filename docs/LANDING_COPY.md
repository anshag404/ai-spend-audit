# Landing Page Copy

> Copywriting analysis and A/B test ideas for AI Spend Audit's landing page.

## Current Copy Inventory

### Hero Section

**Badge:** "🚀 Free for startups · No credit card"
**Headline:** "Stop overpaying for AI tools your team barely uses"
**Subheadline:** "Paste your subscriptions, and we surface redundant tools, usage gaps, and concrete savings — in under 60 seconds. No integrations needed."
**Primary CTA:** "Start free audit →"
**Secondary CTA:** "See how it works"
**Social proof:** "Trusted by 200+ early-stage startups · Average savings $3,400/yr"

### Analysis

| Element | Strength | Improvement Opportunity |
|---------|----------|----------------------|
| Headline | Strong pain point. "Barely uses" is emotionally resonant. | Test: "Your team's AI bill is 40% too high" (specific claim) |
| Subheadline | Clear value prop. "60 seconds" + "No integrations" reduce friction. | Slightly long. Could cut "No integrations needed." to the badge. |
| Primary CTA | "Start free audit" is clear and low-commitment. | Test: "Audit my AI spend" (first person) |
| Social proof | "200+" and "$3,400/yr" are specific and credible. | Add a named company testimonial when available. |

### How It Works Section

**Pre-heading:** "HOW IT WORKS"
**Heading:** "From spreadsheet to savings in 60 seconds"
**Subheading:** "No integrations. No OAuth dances. Just paste, analyse, save."

**Steps:**
1. "List your AI tools" — "Paste in your team's AI and SaaS subscriptions — names, prices, team usage. Takes about two minutes."
2. "We analyse overlaps" — "Our engine maps your stack against 500+ known AI tools, detects capability overlaps, and flags underutilised seats."
3. "Get your savings report" — "Receive a prioritised report with specific tools to cut, alternatives to consolidate onto, and the exact dollar savings."

### Analysis

| Element | Strength | Improvement Opportunity |
|---------|----------|----------------------|
| Section heading | "Spreadsheet to savings" is a great contrast. | — |
| Step 1 | "Two minutes" sets expectations. | "Paste" is misleading — it's a selector, not a paste. Change to "Select your tools." |
| Step 2 | "500+ known AI tools" implies comprehensive data. | We have 10 tools. Change to "our pricing database" or increase tool count. |
| Step 3 | "Exact dollar savings" builds trust. | — |

## A/B Test Suggestions

### Test 1: Headline Variants

| Variant | Hypothesis |
|---------|-----------|
| A (current): "Stop overpaying for AI tools your team barely uses" | Pain-driven, emotional |
| B: "Your startup's AI bill is 40% higher than it should be" | Data-driven, specific claim |
| C: "How much of your $500/mo AI budget is wasted?" | Interactive, makes the reader calculate |

### Test 2: CTA Copy

| Variant | Hypothesis |
|---------|-----------|
| A (current): "Start free audit →" | Clear, standard |
| B: "Audit my AI spend →" | First person, ownership |
| C: "Find my savings →" | Outcome-focused |

### Test 3: Social Proof

| Variant | Hypothesis |
|---------|-----------|
| A (current): "Trusted by 200+ early-stage startups" | Authority |
| B: "Average startup saves $283/mo after their audit" | Outcome-driven |
| C: Quote from a real user + company name | Credibility |

## Copywriting Principles Used

1. **Specificity over vagueness** — "$3,400/yr" beats "save money"
2. **Time investment clarity** — "60 seconds" and "two minutes" reduce commitment anxiety
3. **Negative friction callouts** — "No credit card", "No login", "No integrations" preempt objections
4. **Action-oriented CTAs** — "Start" implies beginning a journey, not a commitment
5. **Pain before solution** — Headline addresses the problem, subheadline introduces the tool

## Recommended Copy Changes

1. **Step 1:** Change "Paste in your team's AI and SaaS subscriptions" to "Select your team's AI subscriptions from our catalog"
2. **Step 2:** Change "500+ known AI tools" to "our AI pricing database" (honest about scope)
3. **Social proof number:** Remove "200+" until real usage data supports it, or change to "Built for early-stage startups"
