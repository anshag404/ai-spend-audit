# Go-to-Market Strategy

> How to get the first 1,000 users for AI Spend Audit.

## Positioning

**One-liner:** "Find out how much your team is overspending on AI tools — free, in 60 seconds."

**Category:** SaaS cost optimisation (subcategory: AI tool audit)

**Target persona:** Engineering leads and technical co-founders at seed-to-Series-A startups (5–50 employees) who manage their team's AI tool subscriptions.

**Why this persona:**
- They control the budget (or influence it directly)
- They understand the tool landscape
- They feel the pain monthly when invoices hit
- They can act on recommendations without approval chains

## Distribution Channels (Ranked by Expected ROI)

### 1. Indie Hacker Communities (Week 1–2)

**Where:** Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, Hacker News (Show HN)

**Tactic:** Launch with a "Show HN: I audited my startup's AI spend — we were wasting $400/mo" post. Include a real audit of your own tool stack (dogfooding).

**Expected reach:** 5,000–15,000 impressions
**Expected conversions:** 200–500 audits completed
**Cost:** $0 (time only)

**Why this works:** This audience loves free tools, has the exact problem, and shares things that save money.

### 2. Twitter/X Developer Community (Week 1–4)

**Tactic:** Create a thread: "We analysed 50 startups' AI tool stacks. Here's what we found." Include real data patterns (anonymised):
- Average startup pays for 2.3 redundant AI tools
- 67% overpay on seat licenses
- Annual billing saves 18% on average

Post the tool link at the end. Pin the thread.

**Expected reach:** 10,000–50,000 impressions
**Expected conversions:** 100–300 audits
**Cost:** $0

### 3. Startup Subreddits (Week 2–3)

**Where:** r/startups, r/SaaS, r/entrepreneur, r/webdev

**Tactic:** Write a genuine "I built this" post explaining the problem and showing results. Reddit rewards authenticity and punishes obvious marketing.

**Expected reach:** 3,000–10,000 impressions
**Expected conversions:** 50–150 audits
**Cost:** $0

### 4. LinkedIn Organic (Week 3–6)

**Tactic:** Target CTOs and VPs of Engineering. Post format: "I just saved my 8-person team $3,400/yr on AI tools. Here's how." Carousel post with before/after audit results.

**Expected reach:** 5,000–20,000 impressions
**Expected conversions:** 50–200 audits
**Cost:** $0

### 5. Cold Outreach to Accelerators (Week 4–8)

**Tactic:** Email YC, Techstars, and 500 Startups batch companies with a personalised offer: "Free AI spend audit for your portfolio companies." Offer to run a 15-minute live audit on a call.

**Expected response rate:** 5–10%
**Expected conversions:** 20–50 audits
**Cost:** $0 (time only)

**Why:** Accelerator companies are the exact ICP. A warm intro from the accelerator multiplies trust.

### 6. Content Marketing (Month 2+)

**Topics:**
- "How much should a 10-person startup spend on AI tools?" (SEO play)
- "Cursor vs Copilot vs Windsurf: 2026 pricing comparison" (buying intent)
- "The hidden cost of ChatGPT Pro" (controversy drives shares)

**Expected monthly traffic:** 500–2,000 organic visits after 3 months
**Cost:** Time to write

## Pricing Strategy

### Phase 1: Free (Now)
The core audit is free forever. No login required. This maximises distribution and builds trust.

### Phase 2: Email Gate (Month 2)
After the audit, prompt: "Enter your email to save your report and get monthly spend alerts." This captures leads for the paid product.

### Phase 3: Premium Features (Month 4+)
| Feature | Price |
|---------|-------|
| Monthly re-audit with trend tracking | $9/mo |
| Team dashboard (multiple auditors) | $29/mo |
| Slack alerts on spend changes | $9/mo |
| PDF export with company branding | $9/mo |
| **Bundle** | **$49/mo** |

### Why This Pricing
- $9/mo is impulse-buy territory for a startup
- $49/mo bundle is cheaper than a single wasted AI seat
- Free tier ensures the viral loop continues

## Viral Mechanics

### Built-in sharing
The results page has "Share on Twitter" and "Share on LinkedIn" buttons with pre-written copy:
- "I just found $X/yr in AI savings for my team. Try it free →"

### Shareable report links
Every saved audit gets a unique URL (`/r/abc123`). When shared, the recipient sees the full report — and a CTA to run their own audit.

### Natural word of mouth
When an engineering lead cuts $300/mo from the AI budget, their CEO asks how. "I used this free tool" is the answer. Zero acquisition cost.
